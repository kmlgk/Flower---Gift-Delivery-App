7 tips to keep your bouquet fresh for longer

Meera Kulkarni

Chief Florist at Bloomora

A fresh bouquet is a little piece of joy — but without the right care, those blooms can fade within days. Our florists put together their favourite tricks to help your flowers (or the ones you send) last as long as possible.

1. Trim stems at an angle

Cut 2-3cm off each stem at a 45° angle using sharp shears. This exposes more surface area for water uptake and prevents the stem from sitting flat on the vase base.

2. Change the water every two days

Bacteria build-up is the #1 cause of early wilting. Rinse the vase and refresh with clean, cool water every other day, adding the flower food sachet each time.

3. Keep away from direct heat & fruit bowls

Ripening fruit releases ethylene gas that speeds up wilting. Keep your bouquet away from fruit bowls, radiators, and direct sunlight.

4. Remove submerged leaves

Any foliage sitting below the waterline should be stripped — it decays quickly and clouds the water.

"The right water, the right cut, and a little love — that's the entire secret." — Meera Kulkarni

5-7. Re-cut weekly, mist petals, and rotate the vase

Give your stems a fresh diagonal cut every 4-5 days, lightly mist petals (not stems) for humidity-loving blooms like hydrangea, and rotate the vase for even light exposure.
